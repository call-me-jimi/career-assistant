Task: Analyse the job description and company context below in light of the candidate profile.

Return a single JSON object with exactly these top-level keys. All values are strings.

```json
{
  "job_title": "<job title as stated in the posting>",
  "company_name": "<hiring company name>",
  "location": "<city or region where the role is based; empty string if not stated>",
  "job_language": "<language the job ad is written in, e.g. English, German, French>",
  "company_description": "<3-5 sentence summary of the company's business, market position, and relevant context, drawn only from the supplied materials>",
  "job_description": "<full detailed analysis — see required sections below>"
}
```

For the "job_description" value, write a detailed analysis using exactly these sections:

1. Core Responsibilities
Identify the 5 most important responsibilities.
Separate:
- Explicitly stated in the job description
- Likely central based on the wording and emphasis of the posting

2. Key Skills and Qualifications
List the 5 most critical capabilities or qualifications.
Prioritise what appears most decision-relevant, not what appears most often.

3. Leadership Expectations
Describe what type of leader the company likely wants in this role.
Address:
- operating style
- level of ownership
- scale or ambiguity
- stakeholder expectations
Label inferred points as "likely inferred."

4. Business Problems This Role Likely Solves
Separate into:
- Problems explicitly indicated by the job description
- Problems likely inferred from the company context and role framing

5. Strategic Priorities of the Company
Identify major business goals, initiatives, or organisational priorities that are explicitly stated in the company description.
Then add any "likely inferred" priorities separately.

6. Data / AI / Analytics / Engineering Relevance
Explain how data, analytics, AI, ML, or platform/engineering capability likely supports the company's strategy and this role.
Use only what is supported by the supplied materials.

7. Important Keywords and Themes
List concepts, priorities, and phrases that should appear naturally in the cover letter.
Prioritise themes that improve perceived fit.

8. Hiring Manager Priorities
Summarise what the hiring manager is probably valuing most in a candidate for this role.
Keep this practical and selective.

9. Potential Candidate Risks or Gaps
Based on the candidate profile, identify likely areas where this candidate may appear weaker, more adjacent, or less directly proven for this role.
Do not overstate risk.
Do not invent missing experience.
Only identify plausible concerns supported by the comparison.

10. How the Letter Should Handle Those Risks
For each risk or gap, explain how the cover letter should address it credibly using supported evidence.
Also note any claims that should be avoided.
