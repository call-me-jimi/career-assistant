Write a cover letter addressed to a recruiter or executive search firm representing an undisclosed client.

Goal:
Position the candidate as a strong senior leadership candidate for the brief while remaining credible and flexible regarding the unknown client organisation.

Use the supplied materials as the only source of truth.
Do not invent experience, scope, domain knowledge, tools, or achievements.
Do not make assumptions about the undisclosed client beyond what is supported by the recruiter ad and inferred role context.

--------------------------------------------------
ROLE INFORMATION

Recruiter or executive search firm name:
{{ recruiter_name }}

Role title:
{{ job_title }}

--------------------------------------------------
OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS

Length:
230–300 words (body only, excluding salutation and farewell)

Format:
4 paragraphs, no bullets

Voice:
First person

Tone:
- senior
- confident without sounding promotional
- concise
- pragmatic
- natural and authentic
- credible to a recruiter or search consultant

Salutation:
Begin with "Dear [Recruiter Name]," if a name is available in the inputs.
Otherwise use "Dear [Recruiter Firm Name] Team," or "Dear Hiring Team,".

Farewell:
End with a closing line (e.g. "Kind regards," or "Yours sincerely,") followed by the candidate's full name on the next line.

--------------------------------------------------
PRIMARY OBJECTIVE

Write a letter that helps a recruiter see the candidate as a credible, presentable match for this brief.

Do not write as if the candidate already knows the client in detail.
Do not write a generic executive cover letter.
Do not optimise for polish over relevance.

--------------------------------------------------
PRIORITISATION ORDER

Prioritise, in order:
1. Clear relevance to the likely leadership brief
2. Leadership scope and ownership
3. Execution credibility and measurable business impact
4. Transferable fit for an undisclosed client context
5. Concise, natural, recruiter-appropriate tone

Use only the most relevant evidence.
Leave out facts that are true but do not strengthen fit.

--------------------------------------------------
PARAGRAPH FUNCTION

Paragraph 1:
- establish seniority immediately
- mention the role title explicitly
- position the candidate as a credible match for the brief
- if recruiter name is known, address the application naturally without inventing familiarity

Paragraph 2:
- show leadership in building teams, setting direction, and driving execution
- emphasise operating ownership, organisational influence, and cross-functional work
- if supported, include platform, production, AI, analytics, or engineering leadership signals relevant to the brief

Paragraph 3:
- include 2–3 outcomes that best prove fit
- prioritise outcomes that reduce recruiter doubt
- integrate metrics naturally where stated
- keep the examples selective

Paragraph 4:
- express interest in discussing the opportunity further
- explain the type of mandate where the candidate is strongest, if useful
- remain flexible and credible given that the client is undisclosed
- close directly and professionally

--------------------------------------------------
SPECIAL GUIDANCE

Because the client is undisclosed:
- do not pretend to know the company's exact strategy, stage, or priorities unless they are clearly supported by the recruiter ad
- do not use employer-specific praise
- keep the letter specific to the brief, but flexible enough for recruiter-led introduction

If the candidate is adjacent rather than exact:
- do not hide the adjacency
- frame transferable relevance through comparable scope, business outcomes, leadership context, and execution discipline
- avoid overstating direct domain expertise that is not supported

--------------------------------------------------
STYLE RULES

Avoid:
- generic enthusiasm
- inflated claims
- abstract corporate language
- phrases that could fit any senior data or AI application
- repeating the recruiter brief in vague terms

Do not use:
- "I am excited to apply"
- "passionate about data"
- "cutting-edge"
- "fast-paced"
- "spearheaded"

Prefer:
- concrete nouns
- ownership language
- quantified outcomes
- plain English
- sentences generally between 10 and 22 words
