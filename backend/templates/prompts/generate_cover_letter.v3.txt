Write a tailored cover letter for the following role.

Your objective is to maximise the probability that a hiring manager invites the candidate to interview.

Use the supplied materials as the only source of truth.
Do not invent experience, scope, tools, domain expertise, or achievements.
If evidence is adjacent rather than exact, position it carefully and credibly.

--------------------------------------------------
ROLE INFORMATION

Company:
{{ company_name }}

Job Title:
{{ job_title }}

--------------------------------------------------
OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS

Length:
260–340 words

Format:
4 paragraphs, no bullets

Voice:
First person

Tone:
- senior
- measured
- commercially minded
- concise
- credible to an experienced hiring manager
- natural, not promotional

--------------------------------------------------
PRIMARY OBJECTIVE

Write a letter that makes the candidate feel like a plausible, high-value match for this exact role.

Do not simply summarise the profile.
Do not write a generic executive cover letter.
Do not optimise for elegance over relevance.

--------------------------------------------------
PRIORITISATION ORDER

Prioritise, in order:
1. Clear match to the role's core leadership problem
2. Leadership scope and ownership
3. Execution credibility and business impact
4. Company-specific relevance
5. Concise and credible tone

Use only the most relevant evidence.
Leave out facts that are true but do not strengthen fit.

--------------------------------------------------
PARAGRAPH FUNCTION

Paragraph 1:
- establish seniority immediately
- mention the Job Title and Company explicitly
- establish current or comparable leadership scope
- show why this is a credible next step, not a random application

Paragraph 2:
- show leadership in building teams, setting direction, and driving execution
- emphasise operating ownership, not abstract strategy
- if supported, include technical leadership, platform ownership, production delivery, hiring, coaching, architecture, or cross-functional delivery signals

Paragraph 3:
- include 2–3 outcomes that best prove fit
- prioritise outcomes that reduce hiring doubt
- integrate metrics naturally where stated
- do not overload the paragraph with too many examples

Paragraph 4:
- explain why the candidate is a strong fit for this specific company and role
- refer to the company context with specificity
- avoid generic praise
- close directly and with restraint

--------------------------------------------------
SPECIAL GUIDANCE FOR ADJACENT FIT

If the candidate is not an exact match:
- do not hide the adjacency
- frame transferable relevance through comparable scope, leadership context, operating rigor, production delivery, or business outcomes
- avoid overstating direct domain expertise that is not supported

If the role is engineering, platform, or production-oriented:
- prioritise evidence of technical leadership, system ownership, engineering standards, architecture decisions, ML or data platform operations, multidisciplinary delivery, hiring, and coaching
- do not let the letter read like a pure analytics or strategy profile unless that is what the role requires

--------------------------------------------------
STYLE RULES

Avoid:
- generic enthusiasm
- inflated claims
- abstract corporate language
- phrases that could fit any AI or data leadership role
- repeating the job description back to the company

Do not use:
- "I am excited to apply"
- "passionate about"
- "cutting-edge"
- "fast-paced"
- "spearheaded"

Prefer:
- concrete nouns
- ownership language
- quantified outcomes
- plain English
- sentences generally between 10 and 22 words
