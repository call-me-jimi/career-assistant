Task: Decide how the candidate should position themselves for this opportunity when applying through a recruiter and the client organisation is not fully disclosed.

Your objective is to help produce a recruiter-facing cover letter that sounds credible, senior, and flexible enough for an undisclosed client, while still feeling specific and evidence-based.

Prioritise experiences that show:
- leadership of teams or functions
- strategic influence tied to execution
- transformation of data, AI, analytics, ML, engineering, or platform capabilities
- measurable business value
- operating ownership and cross-functional leadership

Avoid foregrounding purely technical achievements unless they clearly demonstrate leadership or business impact.

Return exactly the following sections.

1. Fit Type
Classify the candidate's fit as one of:
- Direct fit to the brief
- Strong adjacent fit
- Leadership fit with domain adjacency
- Broad leadership fit, exact mandate unclear
Then explain briefly.

2. Candidate Strengths Most Relevant to This Opportunity
List the 4–6 strongest reasons this candidate is credible for the likely opportunity described by the recruiter ad.

3. Best Achievements to Highlight
Select the 3–4 achievements that most strengthen the candidate's positioning.
For each one, include:
- Achievement
- Why it is relevant to the likely brief
- What concern or doubt it helps reduce

4. Leadership Themes to Emphasize
Identify the leadership themes that should appear in the cover letter.
Focus on themes that remain credible even when the client is undisclosed.

5. Likely Recruiter or Client Concerns
Identify the most plausible reasons the candidate might be screened out, seen as adjacent, or require explanation.

6. How to Address Those Concerns
For each concern, explain how the cover letter should reduce that concern using supported evidence and careful framing.

7. Claims to Avoid
List claims, assumptions, or wording the candidate should avoid because they would:
- overstate fit
- presume facts about the client
- sound too generic
- rely on weak evidence

8. Narrative Strategy for the Cover Letter
Explain the most credible positioning angle for a recruiter-facing application where the client is unknown.

9. Recommended Paragraph Emphasis
Provide guidance for each paragraph:
- Paragraph 1: what it must establish
- Paragraph 2: what leadership and ownership evidence to emphasise
- Paragraph 3: which outcomes to use as proof
- Paragraph 4: how to express interest while staying flexible about the undisclosed client
